About Sony

Sony Group Corporation is a Japanese multinational conglomerate headquartered in Tokyo. It operates as one of the world's largest manufacturers of consumer and professional electronics, a leading video game console company (PlayStation), and a key player in music and film production. Known for innovations like the Walkman, Trinitron TVs, and the Alpha camera system, Sony continues to shape the world of entertainment and technology.

Leadership & Vision

Masaru Ibuka

Co-Founder

"Creativity comes from looking for the unexpected and stepping outside your own experience."

Learn More

Masaru Ibuka, an engineer and visionary, co-founded Sony with Akio Morita in post-war Japan. Their mission was to create innovative technologies that inspire and entertain. Under their guidance, Sony pioneered numerous tech revolutions, from the transistor radio to the PlayStation. Their legacy continues to influence Sony’s commitment to creativity and innovation.
