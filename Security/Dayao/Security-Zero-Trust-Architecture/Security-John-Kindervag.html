About Zero Trust Architecture

Zero Trust Architecture (ZTA) is a modern cybersecurity model that operates on the principle of "never trust, always verify." Unlike traditional security systems that automatically trust users inside the network, Zero Trust assumes that threats can come from both inside and outside. It requires strict identity verification, continuous monitoring, and least-privilege access to ensure that only authorized users and devices can access specific resources. This approach helps prevent data breaches, limits the spread of cyberattacks, and secures remote and cloud-based systems more effectively.

Learn More

John Kindervag is a renowned cybersecurity expert best known as the creator of the Zero Trust security model. With over 25 years of experience in the industry, he rose to prominence during his time as a Principal Analyst at Forrester Research, where he introduced the groundbreaking idea that "trust is a vulnerability." His work redefined network security by proposing that no user or system should be trusted by default. Kindervag has worked with both private and public sector organizations, including government agencies, to help them implement Zero Trust at scale. Today, he serves as the Chief Evangelist at Illumio, continuing to lead discussions on adaptive and secure architectures in an increasingly digital world.
