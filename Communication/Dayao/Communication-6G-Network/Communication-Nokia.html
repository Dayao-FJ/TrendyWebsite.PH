About Nokia

Nokia is a Finnish multinational corporation known for its pioneering role in the mobile communications industry. Originally founded as a paper mill in 1865, Nokia evolved into a major player in rubber, cable, and eventually electronics. Its name became iconic in the late 1990s and early 2000s as it dominated the mobile phone market globally. Though the rise of smartphones led to its decline in handset dominance, Nokia reinvented itself by focusing on telecommunications infrastructure, software, and advanced technologies like 5G, cloud services, and digital health solutions. Today, Nokia stands as a leading innovator in global network technology.

Fredrik Idestam was a Finnish mining engineer, industrialist, and the founder of Nokia. Born in 1838, he established a paper mill in Tampere, Finland, in 1865, which laid the foundation for what would later become Nokia Corporation. Idestam played a crucial role in Finland’s industrialization and is remembered as a visionary who helped transform a small paper business into a cornerstone of global innovation.

Immersive Voice Calls

Nokia Immersive Voice is a cutting-edge audio technology that transforms traditional voice calls into lifelike, three-dimensional experiences. Powered by the Immersive Voice and Audio Services (IVAS) codec, part of the upcoming 5G Advanced standard, this innovation enables real-time spatial audio, allowing callers to perceive voices and ambient sounds as if they were physically present together. By capturing and rendering sound directionality, it enhances clarity and engagement in conversations, making interactions more natural and immersive. This technology holds significant potential for applications in extended reality (XR), the metaverse, and enterprise communications, paving the way for more intuitive and context-rich remote interactions.
